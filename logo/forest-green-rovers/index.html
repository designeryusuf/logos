    Forest Green Rovers Football Club is a professional football club based in Nailsworth, Gloucestershire, England. The team competes in EFL League Two and have played their home games at The New Lawn since 2006, when they moved from their original home at The Lawn Ground. Formed in October 1869, the club became founder members of the Mid Gloucestershire League five years later. Competing in various local league competitions for much of the 20th century, they won a multitude of league titles: the Dursley & District League, the Stroud and District Football League, the Stroud Premier League, the North Gloucestershire League, and the Gloucestershire Northern Senior League. They became founder members of the Gloucestershire County League in 1968, before they were moved up to the Premier Division of the Hellenic League seven years later. Forest Green won the Hellenic League in the 1981–82 season and lifted the FA Vase after beating Rainworth Miners Welfare in the final at Wembley.
